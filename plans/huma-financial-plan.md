## Situation Summary

- Stay-at-home spouse, no current employment income
- 2 rental properties in Texas, managed remotely from San Jose CA
- Filing jointly with Umair (MFJ)
- Goal: Legally shift real estate management into Huma's hands to (1) unlock rental losses against Umair's W-2, (2) generate legitimate self-employment income for her retirement, and (3) build long-term wealth independently

---

## Why This Matters (Numbers)

At Umair's income (~$330k+ W-2), rental losses are **locked as passive** — the IRS suspends them until the property is sold. Huma qualifying as a **Real Estate Professional (REP)** unlocks those losses immediately, saving an estimated **$10,000–$22,000/year** in federal taxes. Combined with a management LLC + Solo 401(k), the total household tax benefit can reach **$30,000+/year**.

## Phase 1 — Foundation (Month 1–3)

### 1.1 Open a Spousal (Backdoor Roth) IRA — **Do this first, it's the easiest**

| Step | Detail |
|---|---|
| Where | Fidelity or Vanguard |
| How much | $7,000/year (2026 limit) |
| How | Contribute to Traditional IRA (non-deductible) → immediately convert to Roth |
| Why backdoor | Umair's income is above Roth income limit — this bypasses it legally |
| Umair should do his too | $7,000 for Umair + $7,000 for Huma = $14,000/year total into Roth |
| Watch out for | Pro-rata rule: if Umair has any pre-tax IRA money, the conversion gets complicated. Keep it clean — use Fidelity and keep both IRAs there |

**Deadline:** Do this before December 31 each year. Can be done in 30 minutes online.

### 1.2 Start the Activity Log — **Most Critical Thing**

The IRS requires **contemporaneous documentation** of hours. A year-end reconstruction is weak. A weekly log kept in real time is audit-proof.

**Set up a Google Sheet with these columns:**

| Date | Property | Activity | Duration (min) | Notes / Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-03-27 | TX Property 1 | Email exchange with tenant re: AC repair | 25 | Gmail thread, 3 emails |
| 2026-03-27 | TX Property 2 | Zillow rent comparison research | 45 | Screenshot saved |
| 2026-03-28 | Both | Reviewed March bank statements | 30 | Chase statements |

**Log this every Friday — takes 10 minutes.** Running total shows in the sheet.

**Target:** 750 hours/year = 62.5 hours/month = ~15 hours/week across 2 properties.

### 1.3 Form the Property Management LLC

| Decision | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **State to form in** | California (not Texas) |
| **Why not Texas** | Huma works from CA. CA will claim she's doing business in CA regardless — forming in TX just adds a foreign LLC registration fee on top of CA's $800 annual minimum. Keep it simple. |
| **CA LLC cost** | $70 filing fee + $800/year minimum franchise tax |
| **Name** | e.g. "Al-Mubarak Property Management LLC" or "HMA Properties LLC" |
| **EIN** | Get one from IRS.gov (free, takes 5 minutes online) |
| **Bank account** | Open a separate business checking account — Chase Business, Mercury, or Relay |

**Management fee to charge:** 8–10% of gross rent collected is market rate in Texas. If combined rent is $4,000/month → fee is $320–$400/month → **$3,840–$4,800/year of self-employment income.**

This income makes Huma eligible for the Solo 401(k) in Phase 2.

**Important:** The rental properties stay in Umair's name. Huma's LLC is paid a fee for managing them. Consult a CPA before moving forward — the structure needs to be clean.

## Phase 2 — REP Qualification (Year 1, Ongoing)

### 2.1 Hitting 750 Hours Remotely

Since properties are in Texas and you manage from California, **everything is remote**. Remote management counts 100% toward REP hours. In fact, digital management is better for documentation because timestamps are automatic.

**What counts — remote management activities:**

| Category | Specific Activities |
|---|---|
| **Tenant communication** | Texts, emails, WhatsApp, phone calls — all timestamped automatically |
| **Tenant search** | Creating/updating Zillow, Craigslist, Apartments.com listings, reviewing inquiries, screening applications |
| **Screening** | Reviewing rental applications, running TransUnion SmartMove credit checks, calling employer/previous landlord references |
| **Leasing** | Drafting leases, reviewing lease terms, DocuSign signing, move-in checklist review |
| **Repairs & maintenance** | Texting/calling contractors, reviewing quotes via email, authorizing work, reviewing invoices, coordinating access with tenant |
| **Bookkeeping** | Logging rent received, categorizing expenses in spreadsheet, reconciling bank account, uploading receipts |
| **Rent collection** | Managing Venmo/Zelle/online portal, following up on late payments |
| **Research** | Comparing market rents on Zillow, reading Texas landlord law, researching repair costs |
| **Property monitoring** | Reviewing Ring/security camera footage, reading HOA meeting minutes, reviewing HOA communications |
| **Insurance** | Reviewing annual renewal, comparing quotes, communicating with insurance agent |
| **Tax prep** | Time gathering rental records, working with CPA on Schedule E |
| **Travel** | Driving to airport for an annual property visit, time at property |

**Weekly rhythm to hit 750 hours:**

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | Check rent received, follow up if late | 30 min |
| Tuesday | Review any maintenance requests, get contractor quotes | 45 min |
| Wednesday | Bookkeeping — log all expenses from prior week | 45 min |
| Thursday | Market research — check comparable rents, Zillow activity | 30 min |
| Friday | Log the week in activity spreadsheet, respond to any tenant messages | 30 min |

That's 3 hours/week × 50 weeks = **150 hours minimum.** Add bigger tasks (lease renewal, finding a new tenant, any repair project, annual property visit) and 750 is very realistic.

## Phase 3 — Solo 401(k) (Year 1–2)

Once the management LLC has income (management fees from Umair's rental properties), Huma can open a **Solo 401(k)**.

### Why this is the biggest retirement opportunity:

| Contribution Type | 2026 Limit |
|---|---|
| Employee (elective deferral) | Up to $23,500 (or 100% of net SE income, whichever is less) |
| Employer (profit sharing) | Up to 25% of net SE income |
| **Combined max** | **Up to $70,000/year** |

At $4,800/year management fee income, her Solo 401(k) contributions are modest (~$4,300 after SE tax). **But as the portfolio grows** — more properties, higher rents — this scales dramatically.

**Where to open:** Fidelity (free, most flexible, allows Roth Solo 401k option)

**Key:** Must be opened **before December 31** of the tax year you want to use it for.

## CA vs. TX Tax Notes

| Topic | What it means |
|---|---|
| TX has no state income tax | Rental income from TX properties is NOT subject to TX state tax |
| CA taxes worldwide income | As CA residents, rental income from TX IS subject to CA state tax |
| Huma's LLC management fee | Also subject to CA state income tax since she earns it while residing in CA |
| The upside | TX property taxes are deductible expenses; TX has no state income tax return to file as a non-resident (only federal Schedule E) |
| LLC formation in CA | One $800/year CA franchise tax fee covers the LLC — simpler than dual-state registration |
